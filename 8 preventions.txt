Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students (India)

Prevention Tips

1.)Use strong, unique passwords for each account and enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
2.)Never share OTPs, passwords, or UPI PINs with anyone, even if they claim to be from a bank. (UPI Safety)
3.)Always verify “collect requests” before approving – remember, you must enter a UPI PIN only when sending money, not     receiving. (UPI Safety)
4.)Avoid clicking on suspicious links in emails, SMS, or WhatsApp messages.
5.)Download apps only from trusted sources like Google Play Store or Apple App Store.
6.)Check your privacy settings on your social media accounts and limit who can see your personal info.
7.)Use secure WiFi networks – avoid logging into your accounts on public WiFi networks.
8.)Keep your devices updated with the latest security patches and antivirus software installed.
9.)Always log out of shared/public computers after use.
10.)Be careful of unknown calls or messages asking for your personal or financial info.

If You Become a Victim

1.)Report immediately on National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)
2.)Contact Cyber Crime Helpline: **1930** (for quick action in financial fraud cases)

If you follow these simple steps, you can avoid cyber crimes and stay safe in today’s digital world.
